# TASTE.md — Pantrio

## What This File Is

This is the aesthetic north star for Pantrio. It answers the question code alone cannot answer: *what should this feel like?* Every UI decision — color, type, copy, layout, motion — should be pressure-tested against this file before it ships.

---

## The One-Sentence Brand

Pantrio is the app that looks at your sad fridge and actually gets excited about what's possible.

---

## Primary References: Oatly + Graza

Two brands. Same energy. Different expressions of it.

**Oatly** — not because we're a food brand, but because of *how* they communicate. Oatly doesn't take itself seriously but it takes its opinions seriously. It talks to you like a person, not a product. The packaging — the UI, in our case — is a place to say something, not just display something. The copy has a point of view. The design has opinions baked in. Witty, human, a little weird, but never random — there's always a brain behind it.

**Graza** — not because we sell olive oil, but because of *how* they designed a commodity into something people feel excited to own. Graza took a product that had no personality and gave it conviction. Bold color (that green), clear hierarchy, copy that's direct and fun without trying too hard. They made something you want to put on your counter instead of hiding in a cabinet. The product *is* the brand. The design *is* the marketing.

Together: colorful, confident, opinionated, human. Designed like someone actually cared, not like something that shipped to hit a deadline.

## The Primary Feeling

**"This is fun, I want to use it."**

Not impressed. Not reassured. Not informed. *Glad I opened it.* The app should feel like it was made by someone who genuinely enjoys cooking and genuinely enjoys design. That enthusiasm should come through without being announced.

---

## What Pantrio Is NOT

These are hard boundaries. If a design decision pushes toward any of these, it's wrong.

- **Not a sterile health/diet app.** No calorie counts foregrounded. No green gradients. No "wellness" aesthetic. No sans-serif-on-white clinical cleanliness.
- **Not a generic SaaS product.** No blue primary buttons. No card grids that look like a dashboard. No "Settings" energy anywhere near the main flow.
- **Not a recipe blog with stock photos.** No hero images of perfectly plated food on marble surfaces. No warm-filter lifestyle photography aesthetic.
- **Not a startup trying too hard.** The personality should feel earned, not performed. No irony for irony's sake. No random quirkiness without substance behind it.

---

## Visual Personality

**Colorful and saturated.** This is not a muted, earthy, "natural ingredients" palette. It's energetic. Colors should feel chosen with conviction, not safety. High contrast. Things that pop. A palette that could work on a poster, not just a screen. Think Graza's green — that level of commitment to a color.

**Type does the heavy lifting.** Typography is not decoration — it's the primary design element. Headlines should command attention. The typeface choices should have character. If you removed all the color from a screen, the typography alone should still make it feel like Pantrio. Big, confident, expressive.

**Layout has opinions.** Not everything centered. Not everything safe. Asymmetry where it earns it. Generous use of space in some places, deliberate density in others. The layout should feel designed, not defaulted.

---

## Copy Voice

This is where Pantrio lives or dies. The copy is half the personality.

**Write like a person, not a product.**
- ✓ "What are you working with?"
- ✗ "Enter your ingredients below."

**Be specific and confident, not vague and hedging.**
- ✓ "Found 6 recipes. Two of them are genuinely good."
- ✗ "We found several matching recipes."

**Empty states have personality.**
- ✓ "Nothing matched. Either your fridge is very sparse or very unusual."
- ✗ "No recipes found. Please add more ingredients."

**Results feel like a recommendation, not a list.**
- ✓ "Here's what you can actually make tonight."
- ✗ "Recipe results:"

**Loading states are not dead time.**
- ✓ "Going through the book..."
- ✗ "Loading..."

The voice is warm, direct, a little dry. It never lectures. It never over-explains. It assumes the user is smart and has a sense of humor.

---

## What Claude Should Do With This File

Before generating any UI component, screen, or copy string, read this file and ask:

1. Does this feel fun, or does it feel functional-but-forgettable?
2. Does the copy sound like a person or a product?
3. Would this fit in a sterile health app? (If yes, redesign it.)
4. Is the typography making a statement or just sitting there?
5. Are the colors chosen with conviction or chosen to be safe?
6. Would Oatly or Graza be embarrassed by this? (If yes, push harder.)

If any answer points the wrong direction, push back and propose something bolder before defaulting to safe.
